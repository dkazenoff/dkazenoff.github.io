Daniel Kazenoff

Hi! I'm Dan, and I'm a senior blockchain engineer at Validation Capital - a crypto infrastructure and staking organization. I have a proven track record of taking ideas to technical execution, and I strive to build experiences that genuinely improves the experience of everyday people. My formal academic background is from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute where I studied mechanical engineering, computer science, and economics. I am incredibly passionate about decentralized consensus technologies, and aim to progress my career with this heavily in mind.

I'm currently the Principal Product Engineer at Validation Cloud. At VC, I am responsible for leading the product vision and implementation for our users. I also monitor and deploy crypto networks onto our cloud infrastructure, and debug various blockchain software to get all of our validator, RPC, and sentry nodes up and running smoothly and profitably. I do a lot of protocol research, partnership-finding, and enjoy closely following activities across the entire crypto developer space and especially EVM. Previously, I was a Decentralized Consensus Fellow at Insight Fellows. There, I built a decentralized cryptocurrency lending platform that contained smart contracts incorporating off-chain KYC to allow for theoretically "zero-collateral" loans, and you can check out the slides here . Prior to that, I worked at RPI's I.D.E.A Lab as an undergraduate blockchain researcher where I built a platform to identify fraudulent crypto wallet addresses with extreme accuracy. I wrote a paper analyzing the findings from this project, and you can check out the published paper here , and the slides here ! Before I graduated from RPI in May 2020, I launched Harbor Apps - a mobile social media platform for lonely RPI students. I also ran Cookies Now, an underground late-night cookie delivery service, and founded RPI's U.A.V. organization.
