Daniel Kazenoff

Hi! I'm Dan, and I'm passionate about making complicated things accessible to everyday people. I strive to build lean software that genuinely improves the experience of everyday people. I am especially excited about decentralized consensus technologies, and hope to continue my career with financial democratization heavily in mind.

I'm now a Decentralized Consensus Fellow at Insight Fellows. I've been building a decentralized cryptocurrency lending platform that contains smart contracts which incorporate off-chain KYC to allow for theoretically "zero-collateral" loans. Previously, I worked at RPI's I.D.E.A Lab as an undergraduate blockchain researcher where I built a platform to identify fraudulent crypto wallet addresses with extreme accuracy. Prior to graduating from RPI in May 2020, I launched Harbor App - a mobile social media platform for lonely RPI students. I also managed Cookies Now, an underground late-night cookie delivery service, and founded RPI's U.A.V. organization.
