Daniel Kazenoff

Hi! I'm Dan, and I'm currently leading Archer Protocol Services. This firm builds produts for the intersection of Web3 and Artificial Intelligence... Archer.Bot is its primary venture, which is translating natural text into on-chain intents. Previously, I led Developer Relations at Validation Cloud - an enterprise-grade crypto infrastructure and staking organization. I have a proven track record of taking strategic ideas to technical execution, and I strive to build experiences that genuinely improves the experience of everyday people. My formal academic background is from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute where I studied mechanical engineering, computer science, and economics. I am incredibly passionate about decentralized consensus technologies, and will not stop until crypto has successfully onboarded billions of users.

At Validation Cloud, I was responsible for leading the developer experience and relations vision. This work was critical for informing our product roadmap as we delivered node infrastructure services to retail developers and enterprises alike. Previously, I was directly on the product team and an infrastructure engineer. I monitored and deployed nodes across a multitude of crypto networks onto our cloud infrastructure, and have debugged blockchain client software to get all of our validator, RPC, and sentry nodes up and running smoothly and reliably I frequently perform protocol research, do partner outreachg, and enjoy closely following activities across the entire crypto developer space. I am not a maxi of any ecosystem, and deeply value the contributions of any protocol that aims to deliver great products and use cases to the next billion web3 users.
I also did freelance web3 development work and AI image infrastructure engineering for Homa Games located in Paris, France. I was critical to the success and deployment of the Valentine NFT collection, as well as deployments of various image gen models for dynamic IP generation use cases. And prior to all that, I was a Decentralized Consensus Fellow at Insight Fellows. There, I built a decentralized cryptocurrency lending platform that contained smart contracts incorporating off-chain KYC to allow for theoretically "zero-collateral" loans, and you can check out the slides here . Prior to that, I worked at RPI's I.D.E.A Lab as an undergraduate blockchain researcher where I built a platform to identify fraudulent crypto wallet addresses with extreme accuracy. I wrote a paper analyzing the findings from this project, and you can check out the published paper here , and the slides here ! Before I graduated from RPI in May 2020, I launched Harbor Apps - a mobile social media platform for lonely RPI students. I also ran Cookies Now, an underground late-night cookie delivery service, and founded RPI's U.A.V. organization.
