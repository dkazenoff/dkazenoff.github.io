Daniel Kazenoff

Hi! I'm Dan, and I'm a graduate of Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute with backgrounds in mechanical engineering, computer science, and economics. I have a proven track record of taking ideas to execution by building out full stack applications, and I strive to build lean software that genuinely improves the experience of everyday people. I am excited about decentralized consensus technologies, and aim to progress my career with financial democratization heavily in mind.

I'm currently working at Validation Capital as a Blockchain Engineer. Previously, I was a Decentralized Consensus Fellow at Insight Fellows. There, I built a decentralized cryptocurrency lending platform that contained smart contracts incorporating off-chain KYC to allow for theoretically "zero-collateral" loans. Prior to that, I worked at RPI's I.D.E.A Lab as an undergraduate blockchain researcher where I built a platform to identify fraudulent crypto wallet addresses with extreme accuracy. Before I graduated from RPI in May 2020, I launched Harbor Apps - a mobile social media platform for lonely RPI students. I also ran Cookies Now, an underground late-night cookie delivery service, and founded RPI's U.A.V. organization.
